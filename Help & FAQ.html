Getting Started

The basics of opening and navigating VPN K

VPN K is a web app with two parts: a VPN connection simulator (for demonstration purposes) and a real private chat service that lets you message other registered users using their email address. The app opens on the simulator screen by default — tap the 🔍 icon to access the chat.
Tap the 🔍 icon in the top right corner of the simulator screen. This opens the login page. There's also a button at the bottom of the screen that does the same thing.
Tapping ☰ at the top left of your inbox opens a sidebar with links to important pages:
  • 📜 Terms of Use
  • 🔒 Privacy Policy
  • ❓ Help & FAQ (this page)
  • ℹ️ About VPN K
  • 📞 Contact Us
  • 🛡️ Security

VPN Simulator

Understanding the connect button, servers, and settings

ℹ️
It's a Simulation, Not a Real VPN

The VPN screen is for demonstration only. It does not change your real IP address and does not encrypt your internet traffic.

The button shows a brief "connecting" animation (around 2 seconds), then switches to a "Protected" status with a connection timer and simulated upload/download data counters. Tap it again to disconnect. None of this affects your actual network traffic.
Open the Servers tab to see a list of 8 countries (Germany, USA, Japan, Singapore, UK, Netherlands, Canada, Australia), each with simulated ping and load values. Tap a country to select it; you'll be taken back to the Home tab automatically.
The Settings tab displays simulated configuration values such as security protocol (WireGuard), ad blocking, dark mode, auto-connect, encryption standard (AES-256), and private DNS (1.1.1.1). These are visual only and cannot be changed.
No. The simulator does not provide real network protection. Do not rely on it for actual privacy or security on public networks — use a genuine VPN service for that purpose.

Account & Login

Signing up, logging in, and managing your account

On the login page, tap "Don't have an account? Sign up," then enter your full name, a valid email address, and a password of at least 6 characters. Registration is completely free.
Each email address can only be linked to one account. If you've already signed up with that address, switch to the login form instead of signing up again. If you forgot you had an account, use that same email to log in.
Gmail addresses (@gmail.com) work most reliably with the authentication system VPN K relies on. Work or institutional email addresses may sometimes be rejected for security reasons, and disposable/temporary email addresses are not permitted at all.
After your first login, the app stores a secure session token on your device. Reopening the app takes you straight to chat without re-entering your password — unless you log out manually or clear your browser data.
Contact our support team at support@vpnk.app with the email address linked to your account, and we'll help you regain access.
Email support@vpnk.app with the account's email address and a request for deletion. Requests are processed within 14 business days. See the Terms of Use page for full details — this action is permanent and cannot be undone.

Chat

Sending messages and managing conversations

Tap ✏️ Start chat at the bottom of your inbox, enter the other person's email address, type your message, and tap ✉️ Send Message.
The person you're trying to message must already have a VPN K account registered with that exact email address. If their email isn't found, the app will show an error and the conversation can't be started yet.
Conversations refresh automatically every 6 seconds. You can also tap the 🔄 icon inside a chat, or the Refresh button in your inbox, to fetch new messages instantly.
Not currently. VPN K Chat only supports plain text messages at this time.

Privacy & Security

How your data and conversations are handled

Only you and the person you're chatting with can see the content of a conversation. The VPN K team may access stored data for technical troubleshooting only, and legal authorities may request data with proper court approval.
No. Messages travel over HTTPS (TLS) between your device and our servers, but they are stored as plain text in our database — not with end-to-end encryption like WhatsApp or Signal. Avoid sending highly sensitive information (passwords, banking details, etc.) through chat.
Yes — your email acts as your public address within VPN K, similar to a username. Anyone who knows your email can start a conversation with you. Keep your Gmail account secure (use Two-Step Verification) since it's tied to your VPN K identity.
We store your display name, email address, and the messages and timestamps of your conversations. We never store your password directly (it's managed by our authentication provider), and we don't collect payment details, photos, files, or your location. See the Privacy Policy page for full details.
VPN K is intended for users aged 13 and older. If you believe a child under 13 has registered an account, please contact us immediately so we can remove it.
