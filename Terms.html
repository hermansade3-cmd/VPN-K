How to Use VPN K — Step by Step

A complete guide from opening the app to sending a message

1
STEP ONE

Open VPN K — Simulator Screen

When you open the app for the first time, you'll land on the VPN K Simulator screen — a dark-themed page resembling a real VPN app. Here you'll see a large round "CONNECT" button in the center of the screen, along with server information and connection status.

2
SIMULATOR

Explore the Three VPN K Tabs

The simulator has three tabs you can tap at the top of the screen:

🏠 Home — Shows connection status, the selected server, and your IP address.
🌐 Servers — A list of 8 countries (Germany, USA, Japan, Singapore, UK, Netherlands, Canada, Australia). Tap any country to select it, then return to Home.
⚙️ Settings — Shows settings such as security protocol (WireGuard), DNS, and encryption (AES-256).

3
SIMULATOR

Try Connecting and Disconnecting the VPN

Tap the large round button with the 🔒 icon. The app will "connect" for about 2 seconds (showing ⏳), then change to 🔓 displaying a "Protected" status in cyan. You'll also see a connection timer, data uploaded/downloaded, and a masked IP address. Tap the button again to disconnect.

4
KEY STEP

Tap the 🔍 (Search) Icon — Top Right

To access the chat section, tap the 🔍 icon located in the top right corner of the simulator screen. This icon takes you directly to the Login page. There's also a small button at the very bottom of the screen that says "Sign in to VPN K Chat" which does the same thing.

5
ACCOUNT

Sign Up or Log In — Login Page

The login page shows an Email and Password form. If it's your first time, tap "Don't have an account? Sign up" and fill in your name, email, and password (at least 6 characters). If you already have an account, simply log in with your email and password. There's also a "← Back to VPN" button if you want to return to the simulator.

6
CHAT

Your Real VPN K Chat Page — Inbox

After logging in, you'll arrive at the main VPN K Chat page. You'll see a list of all your conversations (inbox). There are two buttons at the bottom: 🔄 Refresh (load new conversations) and ✏️ Start chat (start a conversation with someone new). Top left has a ☰ (menu) icon, and top right shows your name along with a logout button (✕).

7
CHAT

Start a New Conversation

Tap ✏️ Start chat at the bottom of the screen. Enter the person's email address you want to talk to (they must already be registered on VPN K), then type your message. Tap ✉️ Send Message. You'll be taken directly into the conversation with that person.

8
CHAT

Send and Receive Messages

Inside a conversation, type your message in the box at the bottom. Tap Enter or the ➤ button to send. Your messages appear on the right in green (📗), and the other person's messages appear on the left in white. The app automatically refreshes new messages every 6 seconds. Tap 🔄 at the top right inside the chat to fetch messages instantly.

9
MENU

Use the ☰ Menu (Hamburger Menu)

Tap the ☰ icon at the top left of the inbox. A sidebar will open from the left containing important pages: 📜 Terms of Use · 🔒 Privacy Policy · ❓ Help & FAQ · ℹ️ About VPN K · 📞 Contact Us · 🛡️ Security. Tap anywhere outside the menu to close it.

10
LOGOUT

Log Out and Return Later

Tap your profile picture (avatar) at the top right of the inbox to log out. Your session will be safely cleared. If you reopen the app, you'll land back on the VPN simulator — this is normal. Tap 🔍 and log in again. If you were previously logged in, the app will remember you and take you straight to chat without logging in again.

Your Data Privacy

How we protect your data

🗄️ Data We Store

  • Your display name
  • Your email address (used as an identifier)
  • Messages sent and received (stored in Firestore)
  • The time each message was sent

🚫 Data We Do Not Store

  • Your password is not stored by us — it is managed by Firebase Authentication
  • Payment or banking information (this service is free)
  • Photos, files, or media content
  • Your geographic location

🔐 Data Security

Your data is stored on Google Firebase Firestore, which uses high-grade encryption. Communication between the app and the server uses HTTPS exclusively.

🍪
Session Storage

The app stores your session (login token) in your browser's localStorage so you don't need to log in again. You can clear this data by logging out or clearing your browser data.

Gmail and Email as Identity

How we use your email address for security

📧
VPN K Uses Email (Gmail) as a Private Identifier

This app does not use a phone number or just a username — instead, your email address (especially Gmail) is your private "address" that allows people to reach you for a conversation. This has a unique security implication you need to understand.

📧 How Your Email Is Used

  • Your email is your public address within VPN K — anyone who is registered can send you a message if they know your email.
  • Your email is stored in Firebase Firestore and may be visible to people sending you messages.
  • Your display name is derived from the part of your email before the "@" if you didn't set a custom name.
  • Changes to your Gmail account do not affect your VPN K account — these two accounts are separate.

🔍 Finding a User

When someone wants to send you a message, all they need to do is enter your email directly into the "Person's email" box. The app then searches Firestore to see if that email is registered. If it exists — the conversation begins. If not — an error message is shown.

🚫 Gmail Address — Special Terms

  • It is strongly recommended to use Gmail (@gmail.com) because it offers the highest reliability with Firebase Authentication.
  • Company or institutional email addresses may work, but Firebase may reject them for security reasons.
  • Disposable/temporary email addresses are prohibited and may result in account deletion.
  • Do not use someone else's email address — this is a violation of these terms and may lead to legal action.
⚠️
The Security of Your Gmail Account Matters a Lot

If someone gains access to your Gmail account, they may also try to log into VPN K if they know the password. Make sure your Gmail has Two-Step Verification enabled at all times for extra security.

Conversation Privacy

Who can see your conversations and who cannot

👁️ Who Sees Your Messages

  • Only you and the person you are chatting with can see the content of the conversation.
  • The VPN K team may access Firestore data for technical and troubleshooting purposes only.
  • Legal authorities may request your data with court approval — we will comply with Tanzanian law.
  • No one else — not your friends, not your family — can see your conversations without your consent.

🔐 Conversation Encryption

All messages travel over HTTPS (TLS encryption) between your device and Firebase's servers. This means anyone attempting to "intercept" your conversations online will not be able to read them. However, messages are stored as plain text in Firestore — not with end-to-end encryption.

⚠️
No End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)

VPN K Chat currently does not use end-to-end encryption like WhatsApp or Signal. Your messages may be viewable by the technical team if there is a legal or technical reason. Do not send highly sensitive information (passwords, bank numbers, etc.) through this app.

🤫 The "Secret" Nature of VPN K

The secret of VPN K lies in its design — the app looks like a VPN simulator from the outside, but inside it is a full chat application. This gives you an extra layer of social privacy: anyone glancing at your screen will think you're using a VPN, not chatting. However, this is not technical security — it is merely security through obscurity. Continue to rely on your usual precautions as well.

Your Account Security

How to properly protect your account

🔑 Strong Passwords

  • Use a password with at least 8 characters (the app accepts 6, but 8+ is much better).
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., VpnK@2025!).
  • Don't reuse the same password across multiple services — this is a high risk.
  • Never write your password anywhere someone else could see it.

🚨 Signs Your Account May Be Compromised

  • You see conversations you did not start yourself.
  • Messages appear "read" or sent without you doing so.
  • The app repeatedly tells you "Session expired" for no reason.
  • Someone tells you they received a message from you that you never sent.
🚨
Immediate Steps If You Suspect Your Account Was Compromised

1. Log out immediately from all devices. 2. Change the password of the related Gmail account. 3. Contact us right away at support@vpnk.app so we can lock that account quickly. 4. Review your conversations — notify affected parties if a harmful message was sent under your name.

📱 Multiple Devices (Multi-device)

You can log into VPN K from different devices (phone, computer, tablet) using one account. Each device will have its own session. Logging out of one device does not affect the others — you'll remain logged in on the others. If you want to log out of all devices, change your password and log in again.
