Overview — What VPN K Is

Understanding the app before understanding the policy

🛡️
VPN K is a Secret Chat App Hidden as a VPN Simulator

VPN K appears as a VPN application on the outside, but it is actually a private messaging platform that uses Gmail (email address) as your identity. The VPN interface is a simulator — it does not provide real network protection. The real functionality is the secure chat system accessed by tapping the 🔍 icon.

🏢 Who Is Responsible for Your Data

VPN K is developed and operated as an independent application. For data-related enquiries, contact us at support@vpnk.app. We act as the data controller for all personal information processed through VPN K.

🌍 Where This Policy Applies

This Privacy Policy applies to all users of VPN K worldwide, regardless of location. It is written in compliance with the principles of Tanzania's Electronic and Postal Communications Act and aligns with internationally recognised privacy standards including GDPR principles.

Gmail Address as Your Identity

Special section: how your email address functions in VPN K

⚠️
Your Email Is Your Public VPN K Address

Unlike traditional apps with usernames, VPN K uses your email address as your unique identifier. Anyone registered on VPN K who knows your email can send you a message. Please only share your VPN K email address with people you trust.

📧 What Happens to Your Gmail Address

  • It is stored in Firebase Authentication as your account identifier.
  • It is stored in a Firestore document so other users can find you by email to start a conversation.
  • It is never sent to any email marketing service, newsletter platform, or advertising network.
  • We never send automated emails to your Gmail address — no newsletters, no promotions, no password-reset spam unless you explicitly request a password reset.
  • We never access the contents of your Gmail inbox. VPN K only uses your Gmail address as a username — it has zero connection to your actual Gmail messages.

🔍 Email Lookup by Other Users

When a user starts a new chat, they must enter the exact email address of the person they want to message. The app checks Firestore to confirm that email is registered. If it is, the conversation begins. No browsable user directory exists — users cannot search for or browse other users. Only exact-email lookup is possible.

Firebase & Google — Third-Party Infrastructure

The technology powering VPN K's backend

🔥 Firebase Authentication (Google LLC)

VPN K uses Firebase Authentication to handle account creation, login, and session management. Firebase Authentication stores your email address and a securely hashed version of your password on Google's servers. We never see your password in plain text.

  • Firebase project ID: smschat-af3e5
  • All auth requests go via: identitytoolkit.googleapis.com
  • Token refresh: securetoken.googleapis.com

☁️ Cloud Firestore (Google LLC)

All messages, conversation threads, and user profile data (name + email) are stored in Google Cloud Firestore. Firestore Security Rules ensure each user can only read and write their own conversations — not those of other users.

📜 Google's Own Privacy Policies

Because we use Firebase (a Google product), Google also processes some of your data under their own policies. We strongly recommend reading:

  • Google Privacy Policy: policies.google.com/privacy
  • Firebase Terms of Service: firebase.google.com/terms
⚠️
Google May Log Your IP Address

When your device communicates with Firebase servers, Google may log your IP address as part of standard server operation. This is outside our direct control. Refer to Google's Privacy Policy for details on how they handle server logs.

Local Storage & Session Data

What we store on your device and why

💾 localStorage — Session Token

VPN K stores one item in your browser's localStorage under the key gmailchat_session. This contains:

  • Your Firebase User ID (uid)
  • Your display name and email
  • A Firebase refresh token (used to get new access tokens without re-entering your password)

🍪 Do We Use Cookies?

VPN K itself does not set any tracking cookies. However, Firebase (Google) may set cookies as part of its authentication infrastructure when running in a web browser. These are functional cookies necessary for the app to work — not advertising or tracking cookies.

🗑️ How to Clear Session Data

  • Log out from VPN K — this deletes the session token from localStorage immediately.
  • Clear browser data — clearing site data in your browser will remove all locally stored session information.
  • After logout, the next app open will show the VPN K Simulator — you will need to sign in again.

How We Protect Your Data

Technical and organisational security measures

🔒 Encryption in Transit

All communication between the VPN K app and Firebase servers is encrypted using TLS/HTTPS. This means your messages, login credentials, and all other data are encrypted while travelling over the internet — they cannot be read by anyone intercepting your network traffic.

☁️ Encryption at Rest

Firebase (Google Cloud) encrypts all data stored in Firestore and Firebase Authentication at rest using AES-256 encryption. Your data is protected even if Google's physical servers were somehow accessed.

📋 Firestore Security Rules

Server-side Security Rules on Firestore enforce that:

  • Each user can only read and write their own conversation documents.
  • No user can read another user's messages — even with a valid token.
  • Unauthenticated requests are rejected entirely.
⚠️
No End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)

VPN K does NOT use end-to-end encryption. Messages are stored as readable text in Firestore. This means VPN K technical staff (and Google, under lawful order) could technically read message content. Do not send highly sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or confidential legal matters via VPN K.

🔑 Password Security

Your password is never stored by VPN K. It is sent directly to Firebase Authentication, which stores only a securely hashed (bcrypt) version. Even our own developers cannot see your password.
